Production Flow

From Raw Paper to
Finished Notebook

Our 8-step manufacturing process ensures every notebook meets exact specifications before it reaches your institution.

Step 1

Raw Material Procurement

Certified 60–80 GSM wood-free paper reels sourced from FSC registered mills.

Step 2

Cutting & Sheeting

Reels are slit and cut to required notebook dimensions using CNC guillotine cutters.

Step 3

Paper Ruling

Sheets pass through high-speed ruling machines applying precise margin and line spacing.

Step 4

Cover Printing

4-color offset printing for covers with GSM board, custom logos and institutional branding.

Step 5

Cover Lamination

Gloss or matte thermal lamination applied to covers for scratch resistance and vibrant finish.

Step 6

Gathering & Binding

Ruled pages are gathered, collated in correct order, and bound using saddle stitch, perfect bind, or stitching.

Step 7

Quality Inspection

100% inspection of bound notebooks for ruling quality, cover alignment, binding strength, and page count.

Step 8

Packaging & Dispatch

Bundled in corrugated cartons, labeled, and dispatched on time to institutions across Tamil Nadu.

Quality Assurance

Our Commitment to
Zero-Defect Quality

Quality is not an afterthought at Century Printograph — it is built into every step of our manufacturing process. Our ISO 9001:2015 certified QC system ensures every notebook that leaves our factory meets the highest standards.

Incoming Paper QC

GSM weight, brightness, and whiteness verified for every paper reel batch before entering production.

In-Process Ruling Check

Line spacing, margin width, and ink density measured every 1,000 sheets during ruling to catch drift early.

Binding Strength Test

Finished books undergo page-pull and flex testing to ensure pages don't fall out under normal use.

Final Visual & Count Inspection

100% visual inspection for cover print quality, alignment, and page count accuracy before packaging.

ISO 9001:2015 Certified

Our quality management system is certified by Bureau Veritas, ensuring international standards compliance in every production run.
